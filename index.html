Software Discovery & Requirements Instrument

Audit Management System
Discovery & Requirements Questionnaire

A structured instrument for the stakeholder workshop with the prospective audit-firm client. Its purpose is to surface, prioritise and document what the audit management system must do — the features, the workflows, the constraints — so the build scope is grounded in evidence rather than assumption.

How to use this document

Work through each section live with the client. Capture answers in the fields directly. For every feature row, agree a priority together — the conversation about why matters as much as the rating. Use the Notes column for caveats, examples and exceptions.

Priority key (MoSCoW)

Each feature is rated for the first release (v1) of the system, not the eventual wish-list.

MUST — v1 fails without it SHOULD — important, not vital COULD — desirable if capacity allows N/A — not needed / later phase

Market landscape — reference

A scan of the tools audit and assurance teams already use, so the client can position what they want relative to what exists. This section is reference material — review it together, then use the questions to capture the client’s reaction.

1.1 The three sub-markets of “audit software”

“Audit management system” means different things in different markets. It is worth being explicit about which one the client is actually asking for — most Ugandan practising firms sit in the first row.

Sub-marketWhat it doesRepresentative toolsFit for a UG practising firm
Financial / assurance Runs the statutory audit engagement end to end: trial balance import, account mapping, risk assessment, electronic working papers, review & sign-off, financial statements and the audit report. CaseWare (Audit / Working Papers + IDEA), CCH Axcess Engagement, Thomson Reuters AdvanceFlow, AuditFile, Inflo Primary fit. This is what most ICPAU-registered firms mean by an audit system.
Internal audit / GRC Runs an internal audit function: annual planning, audit universe, risk-based scheduling, fieldwork, issue & remediation tracking, board-level dashboards. TeamMate+, AuditBoard, Diligent HighBond, MetricStream, Workiva, Resolver, SAP Audit Management Relevant if the firm offers internal audit / risk advisory as a service line, or for in-house IA departments.
Compliance / inspection Runs checklist-based ISO, quality, EHS and field inspections: templates, scoring, non-conformities, corrective actions, mobile/offline capture. Audit Pro (Omnex), AuditPRO.com, SafetyCulture Usually out of scope — but a good reference for checklist, mobile and offline patterns.

1.2 Feature reference — what the established tools commonly offer

Recurring capabilities across the financial-audit and internal-audit tools above. Use this as a prompt list — not every item will matter to this client.

Capability areaTypically includes
Engagement workflowPlanning → fieldwork → review → reporting → archive → follow-up, with configurable stage gates.
Risk-based planningAudit universe, annual plan, materiality, risk register, linking risks to assertions and procedures.
Electronic working papersCentral engagement file, indexed folders, version history, cross-referencing, tickmarks, document locking after archive.
Trial balance & mappingTB import, account grouping to FS lines, auto lead schedules, prior-year comparatives, journal-entry testing.
Templates & methodologyAudit programmes, checklists and document templates aligned to ISA / IIA / firm methodology, updated as standards change.
Findings & CAPAFindings register, summary of misstatements, control deficiencies, corrective actions with owners and due dates.
Review & sign-offReview notes, multi-level review, engagement quality reviews, time-stamped electronic sign-off, full activity audit trail.
Client collaboration“Prepared by client” (PBC) request lists, secure document portal, request tracking.
Analytics & AIFull-ledger analytics / CAATs, anomaly detection, document data extraction (OCR), AI drafting and summarisation.
Practice managementTime & billing, budget vs. actual, resource scheduling, engagement profitability, client database.
Platform & securityCloud or on-premise, role-based access, MFA, encryption, backups, API/SSO integration, mobile and offline access.
Facilitator note. The big international tools are powerful but priced and built for large markets. The opportunity for a Uganda-built system is usually fit, price and support — ISA- and ICPAU-aligned out of the box, priced for SMPs, tolerant of intermittent connectivity, and supported locally. Keep steering the client toward what they need rather than feature-matching the incumbents.
